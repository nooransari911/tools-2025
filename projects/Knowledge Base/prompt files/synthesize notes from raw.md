## Key Components of a Project Plan

- Scope definition: Clearly delineating the Project's boundaries and what it aims to accomplish, thus preventing scope creep.

- Objective setting: Establishing Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound (SMART) goals that guide the Project's direction.

- Resource allocation: Determining the necessary resources, whether they be human, financial, or material, and allocating them efficiently.

- Timelines: Creating detailed schedules, often visualised through Gantt charts or Project Management software, to manage deadlines and dependencies.

## Definitions of Organization, Organization Structure
An organization is an entity where the efforts of a group of individuals are deliberately structured and coordinated to achieve a common set of objectives. It represents the formal system that brings together people and resources to accomplish these objectives, enabling cohesive and effective operation.


Organization structure is the formal framework defining how tasks are divided, resources are deployed, and activities are coordinated within an organization. This framework establishes the reporting relationships, lines of authority, responsibility, accountability, and communication pathways necessary to achieve the organization's objectives.

### Evolution of Organizational Structures
Organizations continuously adapt their structures, which can alter individual roles within both formal and informal systems. Even minor changes can cause conflict. ## Roles and responsibilities of all stakeholders in a project

### 1. Project Manager/Leader

The successful completion of a project is primarily the responsibility of the project manager. Making sure the project meets its objectives within the allotted time frame and budget falls under the project manager's purview. While managing interactions with contributors and stakeholders, project managers ensure that projects receive enough resources.

The following are possible duties for a project manager:
- Drawing out a project plan.
- Controlling outputs in line with the strategy.
- Finding project personnel Managing the project team.
- Establishing the project's methodology.
- Organizing the project's schedule and detailing each step.
- Task distribution among project team members.
- Interacting with senior management.

### 2. Project Team Member

Project team members are those who actively participate in one or more project phases. They could be internal employees or outside consultants who work on the project full- or part-time. The roles of project team members can vary depending on the project. This may include both in-house employees and external individuals who may be consultants, experts, or vendors. The project manager delegates tasks to project team members based on their experiences, abilities and the department they work in within the business.

The following are typical tasks given to project team members:
- Assisting with the overall project goals.
- Completing each deliverable individually.
- Providing knowledge.
- Working together with users to determine and meet business needs.
- Recording the procedure.

### 3. Project Sponsor

The project's sponsor serves as its leader and internal champion. They often belong to high management and are invested in the project's success. Sponsors of a project collaborate closely with the project manager. They contribute to high-level project planning and validate the project's goals. Along with approving the approvals required to take each project phase forward, they frequently assist in resolving disputes and removing impediments that arise during the project life cycle.

The duties of the project sponsor often consist of the following:
- Taking important corporate choices for the project.
- The project's budget being approved.
- Ensuring resource availability.
- Distributing the project's objectives throughout the company.

### 4. Project Stakeholders

Project stakeholders or collaborators include anyone who works toward, funds or is affected by the project's final result. Any project's success depends on the satisfaction of these interested parties.
Project collaborators can be categorized into two ways
- Internal collaborators are those who are directly involved with the project from within the organization. They often include the project team, project manager, project sponsor and other internal teams.
- External collaborators are affected by the project's outcome even though they're not directly involved with the project. They can include external clients, the end-user, customers, subcontractors, the government, suppliers and the community.

The typical responsibilities of project collaborators include:
- Identifying project constraints.
- Naming project risks.
- Offering expertise.
- Giving feedback.

### 5. Business Analyst

The business analyst identifies the requirements of a company and suggests improvements. They ensure that the project's goals meet current business challenges, improve performance, and bring value to the firm through taking part in project teams. Additionally, they could aid in maximizing the worth of the project deliverables.

The business analyst's duties on a project team consist of:
- Aiding in project definition.
- Obtaining requirements from users or business units.
- Technical and business requirements documentation.
- Confirming that the project deliverables adhere to the specifications.
- Testing solutions to confirm goals.
